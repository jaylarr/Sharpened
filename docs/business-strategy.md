# Sharpened Business Strategy

Prepared for: Sharpened  
Phase: Business Strategy  
Primary audience: Social Security Disability law firms  
Future audience: Professional service businesses  
Primary business objective: Generate qualified discovery calls  

## Strategic Position

Sharpened should launch as a specialist workflow automation consultancy for Social Security Disability law firms.

The strongest business advantage is not automation software access. It is the founder's firsthand SSDI intake and filing experience, combined with practical automation and workflow improvement. That operating knowledge gives Sharpened a sharper market position than a generic automation agency.

Recommended market position:

Sharpened helps SSDI law firms reduce repetitive intake, filing, case management, follow-up, and handoff work through practical workflow automation, system improvement, and documentation.

Why:

SSDI law firms have specific operational pain that generic automation providers are unlikely to understand deeply.

How it improves UX:

Prospects immediately understand that Sharpened is relevant to their type of law firm.

How it improves conversion:

Specific positioning builds trust faster and makes the discovery call feel worth taking.

How it improves maintainability:

Messaging, offers, sales assets, case studies, and website content can all follow one business logic.

How it improves scalability:

Sharpened can expand later into adjacent professional service niches after owning a clear first market.

## Business Model

### Recommended Model

Sharpened should operate as a service-led workflow automation consultancy with a diagnostic-to-implementation model.

Business model structure:

1. Diagnose the firm's workflow through a Workflow Assessment.
2. Recommend prioritized automation and process improvements.
3. Implement approved workflow systems.
4. Document and hand off the system.
5. Offer ongoing optimization or support.

### Why This Model Fits

SSDI law firms may not know exactly what needs to be automated. Selling implementation first forces the buyer to make a technical decision before they understand the operational problem. A diagnostic-first model creates trust and protects both Sharpened and the client from poorly scoped projects.

### How It Improves UX

The buyer gets a clear first step that feels lower risk than a large implementation commitment.

### How It Improves Conversion

The Workflow Assessment becomes a natural entry point for cautious law firm decision-makers.

### How It Improves Maintainability

Sharpened can standardize intake, assessment, recommendations, proposals, implementation, QA, and handoff.

### How It Improves Scalability

The same diagnostic method can become a repeatable framework, sales asset, delivery playbook, and future training product.

### Recommended Pricing Posture

Pricing should not be published until packages and deliverables are finalized. The website can describe the assessment and implementation process without listing prices.

Recommended near-term approach:

- Discovery call: free.
- Workflow Assessment: paid or application-based, depending on sales confidence.
- Implementation: custom proposal after assessment.
- Ongoing support: monthly retainer after implementation.

### Why

SSDI firms may have very different systems, case volume, staff structure, and bottlenecks. Fixed public pricing before discovery can either underprice complex work or scare away good-fit prospects with incomplete context.

### How It Improves UX

Prospects understand the path without being forced to self-price a complex operational problem.

### How It Improves Conversion

The first commitment is a conversation or assessment, not a large unknown project.

### How It Improves Maintainability

Sales conversations can follow a consistent ladder instead of custom-selling every engagement from scratch.

### How It Improves Scalability

Once common project patterns emerge, Sharpened can later productize packages with stronger pricing confidence.

## Discovery Call Flow

## Goal

The discovery call should determine fit, clarify pain, build trust, and guide the prospect toward the Workflow Assessment if appropriate.

## Recommended Call Length

30 minutes for initial discovery.

## Recommended Flow

### 1. Opening And Context

Objective:

Set expectations and establish that the call is diagnostic, not a hard sell.

Suggested structure:

- Thank the prospect.
- Confirm their role and firm context.
- Explain the call agenda.
- Clarify that no sensitive claimant/client details are needed.

### 2. Firm And Workflow Overview

Objective:

Understand the firm's size, workflow structure, and current systems.

Questions:

- How does your intake process currently work?
- What happens after a lead qualifies?
- How are filing tasks tracked?
- How does the team manage case status and follow-ups?
- Which systems are used today?

### 3. Pain And Bottleneck Discovery

Objective:

Identify repeated pain, urgency, and business impact.

Questions:

- Where does the team lose the most time?
- What work gets repeated every day or week?
- Where do handoffs break down?
- What communication is most often delayed or missed?
- What creates the most staff frustration?

### 4. Current Attempts And Constraints

Objective:

Learn what has already been tried and what cannot be disrupted.

Questions:

- Have you tried automation or process changes before?
- What worked?
- What did not work?
- Are there systems or workflows the team does not want to replace?
- Are there compliance or confidentiality constraints we should know about?

### 5. Impact And Priority

Objective:

Understand urgency and decision quality.

Questions:

- What would improve if this workflow were smoother?
- What happens if nothing changes?
- Which workflow would you fix first?
- Who else would need to be involved in the decision?

### 6. Sharpened Positioning

Objective:

Connect Sharpened's niche experience to the prospect's pain.

Talking points:

- Sharpened starts by understanding the workflow.
- Automation should improve existing operations, not create disruption.
- The founder has firsthand SSDI intake and filing experience.
- The assessment identifies what should be automated before implementation begins.

### 7. Next Step Recommendation

Objective:

Recommend the most appropriate next step.

Possible next steps:

- Workflow Assessment.
- Follow-up with another decision-maker.
- Request current workflow details.
- Defer if not a fit.

### 8. Close

Objective:

Confirm next action and remove ambiguity.

Close with:

- Summary of pain heard.
- Recommended next step.
- What Sharpened will send.
- What the prospect should prepare.
- Timeline for follow-up.

## Discovery Call Success Criteria

A good discovery call should answer:

- Is this firm a fit?
- Is the pain real and urgent?
- Is there decision authority or access to decision-makers?
- Is the firm open to workflow change?
- Which workflow should be assessed first?
- Is a paid or structured assessment justified?

## Trust Building Strategy

## Trust Barriers

SSDI law firms may hesitate because:

- They handle sensitive client matters.
- They may fear disruption to current systems.
- They may not trust a generic automation provider.
- They may worry automation will make client service feel impersonal.
- They may need proof before sharing workflow details.

## Trust Builders

### Founder-Market Fit

Use the founder's SSDI intake and filing experience as a core credibility point.

### Practical Language

Avoid jargon. Explain outcomes in plain terms.

### Risk Reversal

Emphasize that Sharpened improves and connects existing workflows where possible.

### Confidentiality Boundaries

State that no sensitive claimant/client information is required through public forms.

### SHARP Values

Use Simplicity, Honesty, Accountability, Reliability, and Progress as trust signals.

### Proof Assets

Build and publish:

- Demo workflows.
- Sample assessment output.
- Case studies.
- Testimonials.
- Before/after maps.
- Metrics when available.

### Documentation And QA

Position documentation and quality assurance as part of implementation, not optional extras.
