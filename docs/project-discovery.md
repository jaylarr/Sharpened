## Executive Summary

Sharpened has a strong strategic foundation if it launches with a focused niche: workflow automation for Social Security Disability law firms. The broad brand material describes small-business automation, but the most commercially defensible position comes from the founder's firsthand SSDI intake and filing experience.

The best launch strategy is not to present Sharpened as a generic automation agency. The website should position Sharpened as a practical workflow partner that helps SSDI law firms reduce repetitive intake, filing, case management, follow-up, and handoff work without removing the human connection their clients need.

The strongest conversion offer is the Workflow Assessment. It lowers buyer risk, creates a consultative first step, and gives Sharpened a repeatable method for diagnosing operational issues before proposing implementation work.

The brand is credible, practical, and conversion-ready in concept. The missing pieces are proof assets, final brand assets, final domain/contact details, service packaging, pricing posture, form/booking decisions, and production-ready implementation.

## Business Overview

Sharpened is a workflow automation consultancy. It designs and implements business systems that reduce repetitive manual work, improve operational consistency, and help teams spend more time on higher-value human service.

The business is service-led, not SaaS-led.

Core business posture:

- Sharpened does not primarily sell software.
- Sharpened builds practical business systems.
- Sharpened improves and connects existing workflows where possible.
- Sharpened starts with operational diagnosis before implementation.
- Sharpened should lead with outcomes, not tools.

Likely service areas:

- SSDI Workflow Assessment.
- SSDI Operations Optimization System.
- Intake workflow automation.
- Filing workflow automation.
- Case management workflow support.
- Client follow-up and communication automation.
- CRM and system integration.
- SOP and documentation support.

- GoHighLevel support where relevant, but not as the main brand promise.

## Recommendations

### 1. Lead With SSDI Law Firms

Use SSDI law firms as the homepage audience and primary SEO/conversion focus.

Why:

This is the strongest point of differentiation and credibility.

UX improvement:

Visitors immediately know the website is for their type of firm.

Conversion improvement:

Specificity builds trust faster than broad automation claims.

Maintainability improvement:

Copy, pages, FAQs, CTAs, and proof assets can follow one audience logic.

Scalability improvement:

Sharpened can expand later from a strong niche foundation.

### 2. Make Workflow Assessment The Primary CTA

Use "Book a Workflow Assessment" as the main conversion action.

Why:

An assessment is lower risk than asking visitors to buy implementation immediately.

UX improvement:

Users get a clear first step.

Conversion improvement:

The CTA matches a cautious operational buyer's decision process.

Maintainability improvement:

One primary CTA can be reused consistently across pages.

Scalability improvement:

The assessment can become a repeatable sales and delivery system.

### 3. Build A Proprietary SSDI Operations Framework

Turn founder knowledge into a named methodology.

Why:

The strongest competitive advantage is SSDI operational knowledge, not tool access.

UX improvement:

Prospects can see that Sharpened has a method.

Conversion improvement:

Methodology increases authority and reduces perceived risk.

Maintainability improvement:

The same framework can organize content, sales assets, assessments, and delivery.

Scalability improvement:

Frameworks can become playbooks, templates, training, and reusable IP.

### 4. Keep Tool Mentions Secondary

Mention GoHighLevel and other tools only after the operational outcome is clear.

Why:

Tool-first messaging makes Sharpened easier to compare with generic agencies.

UX improvement:

Visitors focus on their workflow problem, not software categories.

Conversion improvement:

Outcome-led messaging speaks to decision-makers, not just technical users.

Maintainability improvement:

The website remains relevant if preferred tools change.

Scalability improvement:

Sharpened can support multiple systems without reworking the brand.

### 5. Create Proof Assets Before Public Launch

Develop demo workflows, anonymized examples, screenshots, workflow maps, or sample assessment output.

Why:

Law firms will need evidence before trusting an operations partner.

UX improvement:

Visitors can understand what Sharpened actually does.

Conversion improvement:

Concrete proof reduces hesitation.

Maintainability improvement:

Proof assets can be reused across pages, proposals, LinkedIn, and sales decks.

Scalability improvement:

Each future case study can follow the same structure.

### 6. Polish The Team Story

Use the founder/team origin story, but rewrite it for professional service buyers.

Why:

The raw story is sincere but too informal for a production website aimed at law firms.

UX improvement:

Visitors get human connection without losing confidence.

Conversion improvement:

Professional trust supports discovery call bookings.

Maintainability improvement:

A polished story can be reused in About, proposals, and sales material.

Scalability improvement:

The brand can grow beyond a freelancer origin while keeping its mission.

- Minimal approved implementation paths: `/`, `/about`, `/services`, `/workflow-assessment`, `/contact`, plus `*`.
- Expanded IA paths: `/ssdi-workflow-assessment`, `/ssdi-operations-optimization-system`, `/industries`, `/why-sharpened`, `/resources`, `/faq`, `/privacy-policy`.

Why:

Routes affect navigation, SEO, sitemap, internal links, and implementation structure.

UX improvement:

Users get clear, predictable paths.

Conversion improvement:

Maintainability improvement:

Route constants prevent link drift.

Scalability improvement:

Future pages can be added without changing the core funnel.

### 8. Repair Project Hygiene Before Production Expansion

Before heavy implementation, repair Git, align package manager to pnpm, and clean generated build artifacts.

Why:

Production work needs repeatable development and deployment.

UX improvement:

Stable engineering reduces the chance of broken public experiences.

Conversion improvement:

Maintainability improvement:

Clean workflow supports review and future changes.

Scalability improvement:

The project can support more pages, contributors, and deployment environments.

## Discovery Conclusion

Sharpened should launch as a focused SSDI law firm workflow automation consultancy with the Workflow Assessment as the primary conversion path. The brand should be professional, practical, trustworthy, and human-centered. The core message should emphasize that Sharpened reduces repetitive operational work while preserving the human connection SSDI clients need.

The business has a strong niche advantage through firsthand SSDI operations experience. The next strategic priority is not broad expansion. It is sharpening the launch offer, confirming missing business details, gathering proof assets, and continuing implementation only after the current phase is approved.
