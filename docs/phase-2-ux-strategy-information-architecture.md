## Strategic Direction

The website should launch as a focused SSDI law firm workflow automation site, not a broad small-business automation site.

Why: Phase 1 identified firsthand SSDI operations experience as Sharpened's strongest competitive advantage. Broad automation language would make Sharpened easier to compare with generic agencies.  
How it improves UX: visitors immediately know whether Sharpened understands their firm, workflows, and operational pain.  
How it improves conversion: niche specificity builds trust faster and gives the primary CTA a clearer reason to exist.  
How it improves maintainability: content, navigation, CTAs, and SEO can follow one strategic audience instead of serving multiple unrelated markets.  
How it improves scalability: Sharpened can later add adjacent industries from a strong specialist foundation.

## User Flow

### Primary Conversion Flow

1. Visitor lands on Home, Services, or SSDI Workflow Assessment.
2. Visitor confirms relevance: Sharpened serves SSDI law firms.
3. Visitor recognizes pain: intake, filing, case management, handoffs, follow-ups.
4. Visitor sees Sharpened's difference: firsthand SSDI operations experience plus practical automation.
5. Visitor reviews process and risk-reduction language.
6. Visitor sees trust elements: SHARP values, founder experience, demo workflows, future testimonials.
7. Visitor clicks "Book a Workflow Assessment."
8. Visitor completes a short inquiry form or booking flow.
9. Sharpened follows up with discovery call confirmation and next steps.

### Secondary Education Flow

1. Visitor lands on a resource or service page.
2. Visitor reads a practical workflow problem.
3. Visitor follows internal links to the related service or assessment page.
4. Visitor compares Sharpened against internal DIY or generic automation.
5. Visitor books an assessment or saves the resource for later.

### Trust-Building Flow

1. Visitor lands on Why Sharpened or About.
2. Visitor evaluates credibility, team, values, and founder experience.
3. Visitor navigates to Workflow Assessment.
4. Visitor books a call or sends a question through Contact.

Why: the buyer is likely skeptical and operationally busy. The journey must quickly prove relevance, reduce implementation fear, and make the first step low-risk.  
How it improves UX: each path answers a different visitor state: ready to act, researching, or evaluating trust.  
How it improves conversion: multiple paths converge on one primary action.  
How it improves maintainability: future content can be mapped to one of these flows.  
How it improves scalability: paid ads, LinkedIn posts, cold email, and SEO pages can all point into the same funnel.

## Homepage Strategy

The homepage should act as a conversion-oriented overview page for SSDI law firm decision-makers. It should not try to explain every service in depth. Its job is to establish relevance, make the operational problem concrete, introduce the assessment, and route visitors to the right next page.

Recommended homepage section order:

1. Hero
   - State that Sharpened helps SSDI law firms reduce repetitive intake, filing, and case management work.
   - Primary CTA: Book a Workflow Assessment.
   - Secondary CTA: See How It Works.

2. Problem
   - Highlight repetitive follow-ups, signature tracking, handoffs, status updates, and manual coordination.

3. Outcome
   - Explain smoother workflows, faster response, fewer missed tasks, and more staff time for client support.

4. Services Snapshot
   - Summarize workflow assessment, operations optimization, intake automation, filing automation, case management support, and follow-up systems.

5. Why Sharpened
   - Emphasize firsthand SSDI operations experience and practical automation that improves existing systems.

6. Process
   - Assess, map, automate, document, hand off, improve.

7. Trust Elements
   - SHARP values, founder experience, demo workflow placeholders, future proof assets.

8. Primary CTA Band
   - Book a Workflow Assessment.

9. FAQ Preview
   - Address disruption, existing systems, security, timing, and what happens on the call.

10. Final CTA
   - Invite the visitor to start with an assessment.

Why: the homepage must sell the category, niche, and next action quickly.  
How it improves UX: visitors get a clear narrative instead of a scattered agency overview.  
How it improves conversion: every major section supports confidence before the CTA.  
How it improves maintainability: deeper content can move to service, process, and FAQ pages.  
How it improves scalability: the homepage can remain stable while supporting pages expand.

## CTA Strategy

### Primary CTA

Book a Workflow Assessment

Use on:

- Header
- Hero
- Service pages
- Assessment page
- CTA bands
- Footer

### Secondary CTAs

- See How It Works
- Explore Services
- Contact Sharpened
- Read the FAQ

### Future CTAs

- Download the SSDI Workflow Checklist
- Watch a Demo Workflow
- View Case Studies
- Read the SSDI Operations Framework

### CTA Rules

- Avoid tool-led CTAs like "Automate with GoHighLevel."
- Use action language tied to buyer value.
- Keep one dominant CTA per page.
- Use secondary CTAs only when they support the user's readiness level.
- Repeat CTAs after trust-building sections, not only at the top and bottom.

Why: the site needs one primary conversion path.  
How it improves UX: users are not forced to choose between competing next steps.  
How it improves conversion: consistent CTA language builds momentum toward assessment booking.  
How it improves maintainability: CTA patterns can be reused across components and page templates later.  
How it improves scalability: future campaigns can add secondary offers without replacing the primary funnel.

## Content Governance

Every page should follow these content rules:

- Lead with SSDI law firm relevance.
- Explain operational outcomes before tools.
- Use plain language.
- Avoid overpromising.
- Reinforce that automation supports, not replaces, human service.
- Route users toward the Workflow Assessment.
- Use future proof assets wherever claims are made.

Why: consistency is a conversion asset.  
How it improves UX: visitors encounter the same clear message across the site.  
How it improves conversion: repetition reinforces fit and next action.  
How it improves maintainability: writers and developers get a clear content standard.  
How it improves scalability: new pages can be created without reinventing strategy.

## Phase 2 Conclusion

The recommended website architecture is built around one primary buyer, one primary offer, and one primary conversion action:

- Buyer: SSDI law firms.
- Offer: SSDI Workflow Assessment.
- CTA: Book a Workflow Assessment.

The launch site should include Home, Services, SSDI Workflow Assessment, SSDI Operations Optimization System, Industries, Why Sharpened, About, Contact, Resources, FAQ, and Privacy Policy. Future growth should add service detail pages, a proprietary SSDI Operations Framework page, insights, case studies, and additional industry pages only after the core funnel is approved.

The next phase should not begin with UI design until this IA, sitemap, CTA strategy, and page list are approved or revised.
