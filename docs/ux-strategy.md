# Sharpened UX Strategy

## Phase

UX Strategy

## Strategic Objective

Design a focused user journey that turns a qualified visitor into a discovery call request.

Primary audience:

- Social Security Disability law firm owners.
- Operations managers.
- Intake managers.
- Filing leads.
- Practice administrators.

Primary conversion:

- Schedule a Workflow Assessment / discovery call.

Strategic journey:

```text
Visitor
  -> Hero
  -> Problem
  -> Solution
  -> Services
  -> Trust
  -> FAQ
  -> Discovery Call
```

## Executive UX Summary

Sharpened should use a linear, assessment-led journey. The website should not behave like a broad legal software catalog. It should help a cautious law firm buyer quickly answer:

1. Is this for my type of firm?
2. Do they understand my operational problems?
3. What do they actually do?
4. Can I trust them with workflow conversations?
5. What happens if I reach out?

The user journey should move from recognition to clarity to trust to action.

Why:

- It matches how professional service buyers evaluate operational help.
- It reduces confusion by keeping one primary conversion path.
- It prevents Sharpened from looking like a smaller legal tech platform.
- It creates a scalable structure for future services, proof assets, and resources.

How it improves UX:

- Users always know where they are and what to do next.
- The site answers objections in the order they naturally appear.
- The journey respects a law firm buyer's caution around systems, privacy, and disruption.

How it improves conversion:

- Specific SSDI pain builds relevance quickly.
- A Workflow Assessment feels less risky than a full implementation pitch.
- Repeated CTAs reduce decision friction without overwhelming the visitor.

How it improves maintainability:

- Each page has one clear job.
- CTA language can be reused consistently.
- Future proof assets and service pages can plug into the same structure.

How it improves scalability:

- The same journey can later support resources, case studies, industry pages, and other professional service verticals.

## User Flow

### Primary Flow

```text
Qualified visitor lands on Home
  -> Recognizes SSDI-specific workflow pain in Hero
  -> Reviews common operational problems
  -> Understands Sharpened's workflow-first solution
  -> Scans service areas
  -> Reviews trust signals and process
  -> Reads FAQ to resolve objections
  -> Clicks Schedule a Workflow Assessment
  -> Lands on Workflow Assessment page or Contact page
  -> Submits form or books call
```

### Secondary Flow: Service Researcher

```text
Visitor lands on Services
  -> Scans service categories
  -> Selects relevant workflow area
  -> Sees Workflow Assessment as the first step
  -> Clicks Schedule a Workflow Assessment
```

### Secondary Flow: Trust-First Buyer

```text
Visitor lands on About
  -> Looks for credibility and founder-market fit
  -> Reviews SHARP values and SSDI experience
  -> Moves to Workflow Assessment or Contact
```

### Secondary Flow: Direct Contact

```text
Visitor lands on Contact
  -> Confirms what information is safe to send
  -> Completes short qualification form
  -> Receives success message and next-step expectation
```

### Future Flow: SEO Content

```text
Visitor lands on Resource article
  -> Learns about a specific workflow bottleneck
  -> Clicks related service or Workflow Assessment CTA
  -> Enters primary conversion path
```

## Journey Stages

### 1. Visitor

User mindset:

- "Can this company help a law firm like ours?"
- "Is this another software vendor?"
- "Will this disrupt our current systems?"

UX requirement:

- Establish SSDI law firm relevance immediately.
- Avoid generic automation language in the first impression.
- Do not lead with tools.

Conversion requirement:

- Make the Workflow Assessment visible but not forced before context is established.

Recommended page entry points:

- Home.
- Workflow Assessment.
- Services.
- Contact.

### 2. Hero

Purpose:

- Confirm audience fit.
- State the operational category.
- Present the primary next step.

Hero must answer:

- Who is this for?
- What problem category does Sharpened solve?
- What action should I take?

Recommended hero elements:

- Audience-specific positioning.
- One primary CTA: Schedule a Workflow Assessment.
- One secondary CTA: View Services or See What We Assess.
- Short credibility cue: SSDI workflow experience, workflow-first, no forced system replacement.

Why:

- The hero is the highest-attention moment.
- A clear first impression prevents unqualified exploration.
- Law firm buyers need relevance before they need service detail.

### 3. Problem

Purpose:

- Create recognition.
- Name specific operational friction.
- Show that Sharpened understands SSDI workflows.

Problems to cover:

- Repetitive intake follow-up.
- Slow lead response.
- Missing document and signature tracking.
- Filing handoff delays.
- Manual case status updates.
- Inconsistent internal task ownership.
- Staff time lost to repeat admin work.

UX requirement:

- Use concrete workflow examples.
- Avoid fear-heavy language.
- Show operational empathy without exaggeration.

Conversion requirement:

- Bridge from problem recognition to assessment.

Recommended CTA after this stage:

- See How the Assessment Works.

### 4. Solution

Purpose:

- Explain Sharpened's workflow-first method.
- Differentiate from SaaS platforms and generic automation vendors.
- Reduce fear of disruption.

Solution should communicate:

- Sharpened starts with workflow diagnosis.
- Automation is recommended only where it improves operations.
- Existing systems can often be improved or connected.
- Human judgment and client care stay central.

UX requirement:

- Use a simple process narrative.
- Explain current-state to future-state improvement.
- Avoid technical implementation detail too early.

Conversion requirement:

- Make the Workflow Assessment feel like the natural first step.

Recommended CTA after this stage:

- Schedule a Workflow Assessment.

### 5. Services

Purpose:

- Show scope without overwhelming the visitor.
- Let different buyers find their pain area.
- Reinforce that the assessment directs implementation.

Recommended service categories:

- Workflow Assessment.
- Intake Workflow Automation.
- Filing Workflow Automation.
- Case Management Workflow Support.
- Client Follow-Up and Communication Automation.
- SOP and Documentation Systems.
- Ongoing Workflow Optimization.

UX requirement:

- Present services as operational outcomes, not tool features.
- Keep descriptions short and scannable.
- Use the Workflow Assessment as the gateway.

Conversion requirement:

- Each service should point back to the assessment or contact path.

Recommended CTA after this stage:

- Find the Right Workflow to Fix First.

### 6. Trust

Purpose:

- Reduce perceived risk.
- Establish founder-market fit.
- Make Sharpened credible before case studies exist.

Trust elements to include:

- Founder experience in SSDI intake and filing workflows.
- SHARP values: Simplicity, Honesty, Accountability, Reliability, Progress.
- Clear process and deliverables.
- Sample workflow maps or assessment artifacts when available.
- Confidentiality and privacy boundary.
- "Works with your current tools" reassurance.
- Future testimonials and case studies.

UX requirement:

- Make trust tangible.
- Avoid vague claims such as "trusted partner" without proof.
- Do not ask for sensitive claimant/client information in public forms.

Conversion requirement:

- Remove objections before asking for the call again.

Recommended CTA after this stage:

- Discuss Your Workflow.

### 7. FAQ

Purpose:

- Resolve final objections.
- Clarify scope, process, privacy, and fit.
- Reduce unnecessary sales friction.

Recommended FAQ topics:

- Who is Sharpened for?
- Is Sharpened a software platform?
- Do firms need to replace current systems?
- What happens during a Workflow Assessment?
- What workflows can be reviewed?
- Is sensitive client data required?
- What happens after the assessment?
- Does Sharpened work with existing tools?
- How long does the first call take?
- Is pricing public?

UX requirement:

- Keep answers direct.
- Use plain language.
- Avoid legal/compliance overclaims.

Conversion requirement:

- End the FAQ with the discovery call CTA.

Recommended CTA after this stage:

- Schedule a Workflow Assessment.

### 8. Discovery Call

Purpose:

- Convert qualified interest into a conversation.
- Collect enough context to determine fit.
- Set expectation for the next step.

Discovery call page/form should clarify:

- The call is for workflow fit and problem discovery.
- No sensitive claimant/client details should be submitted.
- Sharpened will review the firm's workflow challenges at a high level.
- The next step may be a Workflow Assessment.

Recommended form fields:

- Name.
- Email.
- Firm name.
- Role.
- Website, optional.
- Primary workflow challenge.
- Current tools, optional.
- Message, optional.

UX requirement:

- Keep the form short.
- Use helper text to discourage sensitive client details.
- Confirm what happens after submission.

Conversion requirement:

- Reduce anxiety and make follow-up expectations clear.

## Navigation

## Launch Navigation

Recommended primary navigation:

- Home: `/`
- Services: `/services`
- Workflow Assessment: `/workflow-assessment`
- About: `/about`
- Contact: `/contact`

Primary nav CTA:

- Schedule Assessment -> `/workflow-assessment`

Mobile navigation:

- Same items.
- CTA should remain prominent.
- Avoid deep dropdowns at launch.

Why:

- The launch site is not broad enough to justify complex navigation.
- Simple navigation protects attention and pushes users toward the conversion path.
- A dedicated assessment route reinforces the main offer.

How it improves UX:

- Visitors can predict where each link goes.
- Mobile users avoid nested menus.
- The highest-intent path is always available.

How it improves conversion:

- The Workflow Assessment stays visible on every page.
- The site avoids splitting intent across too many options.

How it improves maintainability:

- A small nav is easier to manage during launch.
- Future pages can be added after proof and content volume exist.

How it improves scalability:

- Resources, case studies, and industry pages can be added later without changing the core conversion path.

## CTA Strategy

### Primary CTA

Use:

- Schedule a Workflow Assessment

Why:

- It matches the business model.
- It is more accurate than "Book a Demo."
- It sets a consultative expectation.
- It makes the first step concrete.

### Secondary CTAs

Use selectively:

- View Services.
- See What We Assess.
- Discuss Your Workflow.
- Contact Sharpened.

### Avoid

- Get Started, unless the destination is clear.
- Book a Demo, unless there is an actual demo.
- Learn More as a primary CTA.
- Multiple competing CTA colors.

### CTA Placement

Recommended CTA placement:

- Header: primary CTA.
- Hero: primary and secondary CTA.
- After Problem: assessment-related secondary CTA.
- After Solution: primary CTA.
- After Services: primary CTA.
- After Trust: softer CTA.
- After FAQ: primary CTA.
- Footer: primary CTA plus contact link.

### CTA Color Rule

Yellow `#FEC001` should be used only for primary CTAs.

Why:

- UX improves because yellow becomes a reliable action signal.
- Conversion improves because the main action has visual priority.
- Maintainability improves because the design system has a strict usage rule.
- Scalability improves because future pages can preserve the same conversion hierarchy.

## Conversion Funnel

### Funnel Overview

```text
Awareness
  -> Relevance
  -> Problem Recognition
  -> Solution Clarity
  -> Service Fit
  -> Trust
  -> Objection Handling
  -> Discovery Call
  -> Workflow Assessment
  -> Implementation Proposal
```

### Funnel Stages

| Stage | User Question | Website Job | Success Signal |
|---|---|---|---|
| Awareness | "Who is this?" | Establish brand and audience fit | Visitor stays past hero |
| Relevance | "Is this for our firm?" | Name SSDI law firms directly | Visitor scrolls or clicks services |
| Problem Recognition | "Do they understand our pain?" | Show specific workflow bottlenecks | Visitor reaches solution/services |
| Solution Clarity | "What do they do?" | Explain workflow-first assessment | CTA clicks increase |
| Service Fit | "Can they help with our issue?" | Map services to operational outcomes | Service page engagement |
| Trust | "Can we trust them?" | Show experience, process, privacy boundaries | Assessment page visits |
| Objection Handling | "What happens next?" | Answer FAQ and clarify process | Form starts |
| Discovery Call | "Is this worth discussing?" | Keep conversion low-friction | Form submission / booking |

### Conversion Principles

1. One primary action.
2. Niche-specific messaging.
3. Workflow-first explanation.
4. No forced system replacement.
5. Privacy-safe contact forms.
6. Specific proof over vague claims.
7. Repeated but contextual CTAs.
8. Short forms.
9. Clear next-step expectation.
10. Future analytics tied to funnel stage.

## Missing UX Decisions

The following items should be confirmed before final implementation:

1. Is the primary CTA label "Schedule a Workflow Assessment" or "Book a Workflow Assessment"?
2. Will `/workflow-assessment` contain a booking embed, a form, or both?
3. What booking tool will be used?
4. What email or CRM receives inquiries?
5. What is the promised response time?
6. Is the Workflow Assessment free, paid, or application-based?
7. Can Sharpened publish sample assessment artifacts?
8. Can founder SSDI experience be described publicly?
9. What privacy language should appear on forms?
10. Should pricing expectations be mentioned at all?

## Recommendations

1. Use `/workflow-assessment` as the primary conversion page.
2. Keep the initial navigation to five links plus a CTA.
3. Use "Schedule a Workflow Assessment" as the primary CTA.
4. Make the homepage follow the requested sequence: Hero, Problem, Solution, Services, Trust, FAQ, Discovery Call.
5. Use problem examples specific to SSDI workflows.
6. Avoid SaaS-style "Book a Demo" language.
7. Add privacy reassurance before every form.
8. Build the Contact form for qualification, not long intake.
9. Add future Resources only after there is enough content to support SEO.
10. Create sample assessment artifacts to strengthen trust before case studies exist.

## Final UX Strategy

Sharpened's website should guide visitors through a focused decision path:

```text
Recognize the SSDI workflow problem
  -> understand Sharpened's workflow-first solution
  -> see relevant services
  -> build trust
  -> resolve objections
  -> schedule a discovery call / Workflow Assessment
```

This strategy keeps the website simple, credible, and conversion-focused. It gives law firm buyers enough clarity to take the next step without forcing them to evaluate a complex product, understand technical tools, or commit to a large implementation before diagnosis.
