# Sharpened Content Architecture

## Phase

Content Architecture

## Purpose

Create the complete website content structure for Sharpened without writing final marketing copy.

This document defines:

- Every launch page.
- Every future/support page.
- Every section.
- Working headline intent.
- Working subheading intent.
- CTA placement and label direction.
- FAQ topics.
- Trust elements.
- Content hierarchy.

## Important Content Rule

The headline and subheading entries below are not final marketing copy. They are structural labels and copy-direction placeholders. Final persuasive copy should be written in a later copywriting phase after client approval.

Why:

- It prevents premature copy decisions.
- It keeps strategy separate from execution.
- It gives designers and developers a clear content map.
- It allows future copywriting to stay aligned with UX and conversion goals.

## Website Scope

## Launch Pages

| Page | URL | Role |
|---|---|---|
| Home | `/` | Primary positioning and conversion journey |
| Services | `/services` | Service education and offer framing |
| Workflow Assessment | `/workflow-assessment` | Primary conversion page |
| About | `/about` | Trust, founder-market fit, values |
| Contact | `/contact` | Lead capture and discovery call entry |
| Privacy Policy | `/privacy-policy` | Required trust and data handling support |
| Not Found | `*` | Recovery path for broken or mistyped URLs |

## Future Pages

| Page | URL | Timing |
|---|---|---|
| Resources | `/resources` | After launch content exists |
| Case Studies | `/case-studies` | After proof assets exist |
| FAQ | `/faq` | If FAQ grows beyond page sections |
| SSDI Workflow Library | `/ssdi-workflow-library` | After framework matures |
| Industries | `/industries` | After expansion beyond SSDI is approved |

Why:

- UX improves because launch visitors get a simple path.
- Conversion improves because traffic is not split across too many pages.
- Maintainability improves because every page has a clear job.
- Scalability improves because future content has a defined expansion path.

## Global Footer

### Footer Content Blocks

Brand block:

- Logo or wordmark.
- Short positioning summary placeholder.
- Primary audience mention.

Navigation block:

- Home.
- Services.
- Workflow Assessment.
- About.
- Contact.

Trust block:

- Privacy note.
- No sensitive claimant/client details through public forms.
- Workflow-first positioning.

CTA block:

- Headline intent: final prompt to discuss workflow.
- CTA: Schedule a Workflow Assessment.

Legal block:

- Copyright.
- Privacy Policy link.

Why:

- UX improves because users can recover and navigate at the end of any page.
- Conversion improves because the footer repeats the primary action.
- Maintainability improves because the same footer supports every route.
- Scalability improves because future pages can be added without redesigning the footer.
