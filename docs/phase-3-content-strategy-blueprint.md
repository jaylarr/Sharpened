## Scope

This document defines the content architecture for the Sharpened website. It does not include final marketing copy, UI layout, visual design, or code.

The recommended content system stays focused on SSDI law firms, with the SSDI Workflow Assessment as the primary conversion offer.

## Global Content Principles

- Lead with SSDI law firm relevance.
- Explain operational outcomes before tools.
- Use plain, practical language.
- Avoid buzzwords and technical overload.
- Position automation as support for human service, not a replacement for human care.
- Reinforce that Sharpened improves and connects existing systems where possible.
- Use the primary CTA consistently: Book a Workflow Assessment.
- Treat proof claims carefully until case studies, metrics, or testimonials are available.
- Keep the tone professional, trustworthy, helpful, clear, and confident.

Why: consistent content prevents the site from sounding like a generic automation agency.  
How it improves UX: visitors get a predictable, easy-to-follow story across every page.  
How it improves conversion: repeated relevance and risk reduction build confidence before the CTA.  
How it improves maintainability: future writers can follow a defined content model.  
How it improves scalability: new pages can be added without reinventing messaging rules.

## Global Messaging Architecture

### Primary Audience

SSDI law firm owners, partners, operations managers, intake managers, filing leads, case management leaders, and practice administrators.

### Primary Message

Sharpened helps SSDI law firms reduce repetitive intake, filing, and case management work through practical workflow automation and system integration.

### Supporting Messages

- Your team should spend less time on routine administrative work.
- Better workflows help law firms respond faster and operate more consistently.
- Automation should improve existing operations without removing human connection.
- Sharpened understands SSDI operations from firsthand experience.
- The assessment is the lowest-risk first step.

### Proof Themes

- Firsthand SSDI intake and filing experience.
- SHARP values: Simplicity, Honesty, Accountability, Reliability, Progress.
- Practical workflow mapping.
- Existing-system improvement.
- Documentation, QA, and handoff.
- Future testimonials, demo workflows, before-after maps, screenshots, and metrics.

## Content Hierarchy Model

Every core page should follow this hierarchy unless its page purpose requires a shorter utility structure:

1. Relevance: who this page is for.
2. Problem: what operational pain the visitor recognizes.
3. Solution: how Sharpened approaches the problem.
4. Benefit: what improves for the law firm and team.
5. Process: what happens next.
6. Trust: why Sharpened is credible.
7. Action: what the visitor should do.

Why: operational buyers need to recognize fit before they care about services.  
How it improves UX: users can scan from problem to solution to action.  
How it improves conversion: the CTA appears after sufficient context and trust.  
How it improves maintainability: each page has a reusable narrative order.  
How it improves scalability: service and resource pages can follow the same pattern.
